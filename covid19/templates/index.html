Project Philosophy

Our information supply is contaminated. In an era where generating content and publishing it is so easy, we are bombarded with irrelevant, redundant, unsolicited, incorrect, and otherwise low-value information, and some of it does not rank high on the truth scale. Our long term goal is to define and address some of the key research questions raised by the need to navigate our way through the information polluted space â€“ in politics, the medical domain, education, and more.

Immediate Goals

In the context of COVID-19, we would like to provide a reasonably-sized set of relevant, trustworthy, and timely information that would allow users to, rather quickly, become informed about the specific topics of interest to them. Our immediate goals are to --

  • Reduce information redundancy
  • Provide a way for users to understand the relations between independently written documents.

To help critical readers become informed about a topic, it is important to expose them to relevant perspectives on a topic while minimizing the superfluous and redundant information. At the same time, it is important to expose and present relations between independently produced documents so that it is possible to evaluate the various emphases and nuances. Moreover, organizing the information available along the topics it addresses, the type of the source generating it, and the document type (e.g, factual reporting vs. opinion) helps the reader approach the subject in an orderly and efficient way.

As researchers in Natural Language Understanding, we seek automated ways, driven by machine learning and inference methods, to understand the documents well enough so that we can support these objectives.
